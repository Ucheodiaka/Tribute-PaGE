Martin Luther King Jr.

The Reverend

1st President of the Southern Christian Leadership Conference

Here's a time line of Martin Luther King Jr.

  • 1929 - Born in Atlanta, Georgia

  • 1935 - Started School in September

  • 1940 - Enrolled at Atlanta University Laboratory School

  • 1942 - Became the Youngest Assistant Manager of a Newspaper

  • 1944 - King gave his First Public Speech

  • 1947 - King chose to enter the Ministry

  • 1948 - King Graduated from Morehouse

  • 1951 - King began Doctoral Studies

  • 1953 - King Married Coretta Scott

  • 1954 - King was Called as Pastor Dexter Avenue Baptist Church

  • 1957 - Kings and others Civil Right activists founded the Southern Chrisitian Leadership Conference (SCLC)

  • 1961 - The Albany Movement

  • 1963 - The march on Washington

  • 1966 - Chicago Open Houseing Movement

  • 1964 - King became the youngest winner of the Nonel Peace Prize

  • 1966 - Chicago open housing Movement

  • 1968 - KIng and SCLC organized the Poor People's Campaign

  • 1968 - Died at the age of 39



    • "Martin Luther King, Jr. was a social activist and Baptist minister who played a key role in the American civil rights movement from the mid-1950s until his assassination in 1968. King sought equality and human rights for African Americans, the economically disadvantaged and all victims of injustice through peaceful protest."
